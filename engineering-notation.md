## Introduction

Engineers and scientists deal with numbers that span enormous ranges — from the size of an atom (0.0000000001 m) to the distance to a star (9,460,730,472,580,800 m). Scientific and engineering notation makes these numbers manageable.

This guide covers how to work with scientific notation on the calculator at [scientificcalculators.net](https://www.scientificcalculators.net), plus a full walkthrough of the built-in **Unit Converter** tool which handles 9 measurement categories with instant bidirectional conversion.

## Scientific Notation on the Calculator

### How Numbers Are Displayed

When a result is very large or very small, the calculator automatically displays it in scientific notation:

`6,022,000,000,000,000,000,000,000` appears as `6.022 E 23`

This means **6.022 × 10²³**.

### Entering Numbers in Scientific Notation

Use the **`10^x`** key on the bottom row of the calculator:

**To enter 4.7 × 10⁸:**
1. Press `4.7`
2. Press `10^x`
3. Press `8` then `=`

**To enter 3.2 × 10⁻⁵:**
1. Press `3.2`
2. Press `10^x`
3. Press `±` (to make the exponent negative)
4. Press `5` then `=`

## SI Prefixes Reference

These prefixes align with engineering notation — each one is a multiple of 3 powers of 10:

| Prefix | Symbol | Power | Value |
|--------|--------|-------|-------|
| Tera | T | 10¹² | 1,000,000,000,000 |
| Giga | G | 10⁹ | 1,000,000,000 |
| Mega | M | 10⁶ | 1,000,000 |
| Kilo | k | 10³ | 1,000 |
| (base) | — | 10⁰ | 1 |
| Milli | m | 10⁻³ | 0.001 |
| Micro | μ | 10⁻⁶ | 0.000001 |
| Nano | n | 10⁻⁹ | 0.000000001 |
| Pico | p | 10⁻¹² | 0.000000000001 |

## Calculating with Scientific Notation

**Multiplying:** `(3 × 10⁴) × (2 × 10³)`

On the calculator: `3 10^x 4 × 2 10^x 3 =` → `6 × 10⁷`

**Dividing:** `(8.4 × 10⁹) ÷ (2 × 10³)`

On the calculator: `8.4 10^x 9 ÷ 2 10^x 3 =` → `4.2 × 10⁶`

**Adding:** Always convert to the same power of 10 first, or let the calculator handle it directly.

## The 9 Converter Categories

### 1. Temperature
Convert between Celsius, Fahrenheit, and Kelvin.

Key examples:
- `100°C` = `212°F` (boiling point of water)
- `0°C` = `32°F` (freezing point)
- `37°C` = `98.6°F` (body temperature)
- `0 K` = `−273.15°C` (absolute zero)

Formulas for reference:
- °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32
- °C = (°F − 32) × 5/9
- K = °C + 273.15

### 2. Length
Convert between mm, cm, m, km, inches, feet, yards, and miles.

Key examples:
- `5 km` = `3.10686 miles`
- `1 inch` = `2.54 cm`
- `1 foot` = `0.3048 m`
- `1 mile` = `1.60934 km`

### 3. Mass / Weight
Convert between mg, g, kg, ounces, pounds, and tons.

Key examples:
- `75 kg` = `165.347 pounds`
- `1 kg` = `2.20462 pounds`
- `1 ounce` = `28.3495 g`

### 4. Volume
Convert between ml, litres, gallons, pints, cups, and fluid ounces.

Key examples:
- `2 litres` = `0.528344 gallons`
- `1 pint` = `568.261 ml` (UK pint)
- `1 cup` = `236.588 ml`

### 5. Speed
Convert between m/s, km/h, mph, and knots.

Key examples:
- `60 km/h` = `16.6667 m/s`
- `60 mph` = `96.5606 km/h`
- `1 knot` = `1.852 km/h`

### 6. Time
Convert between milliseconds, seconds, minutes, hours, days, weeks, months, and years.

Key examples:
- `2 hours` = `120 minutes`
- `1 day` = `86,400 seconds`
- `1 year` = `365 days`

### 7. Energy
Convert between Joules, kJ, calories, kcal, Wh, and kWh.

Key examples:
- `500 Joules` = `119.503 calories`
- `1 kWh` = `3,600,000 Joules`
- `1 kcal` = `4,184 Joules`

### 8. Area
Convert between mm², cm², m², km², in², ft², acres, and mi².

Key examples:
- `100 m²` = `1076.39 ft²`
- `1 acre` = `4,046.86 m²`
- `1 km²` = `247.105 acres`

### 9. Pressure
Convert between Pa, kPa, bar, atm, and psi.

Key examples:
- `101.325 kPa` = `1 atm` (standard atmospheric pressure)
- `1 bar` = `14.5038 psi`
- `1 atm` = `14.6959 psi`

## Tips for Accurate Conversions

- The converter handles very large or very small numbers using scientific notation automatically
- You can type in **either** the From or To field — conversion works both ways
- If a unit seems missing, double-check you have the right **category** selected
- Use the **swap button** to quickly reverse a conversion without retyping
